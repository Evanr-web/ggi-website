# Welcome Email Series — Magnalia Letter

## Overview

**Trigger:** New subscriber joins the Magnalia Letter list (any form on site)
**Platform:** ActiveCampaign automation
**Emails:** 5 over 21 days
**Goal:** Move a subscriber from "interested stranger" to "engaged participant"

---

## Segmentation Strategy

### Entry Points → Tags

When someone subscribes, tag them by source:

| Entry Point | AC Tag | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Homepage newsletter form | `source:homepage` | General interest |
| Magnalia Letter page | `source:magnalia-letter` | Content-driven |
| Event registration | `source:event:{event-name}` | Program interest |
| Reading List download | `source:lead-magnet:reading-list` | Intellectual depth |
| Magnalia excerpt download | `source:lead-magnet:magnalia-excerpt` | Publication interest |
| Book Study signup | `source:program:book-study` | Formation interest |
| YouTube / external | `source:youtube` | Discovery |
| Ambassador referral | `source:referral:ambassador` | Warm intro |

### Email 4 Variants

Email 4 (Community & Programs) has three variants based on tags:

1. **Default** — broad programs overview (homepage, general, ambassador)
2. **Event/Program** — emphasizes the event they registered for + related programs
3. **Content/Intellectual** — emphasizes Book Studies, Library, Fellows (YouTube, reading list, Magnalia excerpt)

Emails 1, 2, 3, and 5 are the same for everyone.

### Email 2 — The Founder's Story
**Day:** 3
**From:** Dr. Ryan Topping <rtopping@gregorythegreat.ca>
**Format:** Plain text (personal letter)
**Subject line options:**
- Why I started the Gregory the Great Institute
- From Mennonite to Catholic — and why it led here
- A personal note from our founder

---

I want to tell you why this Institute exists — and it starts with a funeral.

In 2005, my wife Anna and I were studying at Oxford. We had grown up Mennonite in the Canadian prairies — Saskatchewan and British Columbia. I'd trained to be a Mennonite pastor, studied classics and philosophy, and spent years searching for the intellectual tradition that could hold together everything I believed about God, reason, and culture.

Then John Paul II died. We went to Rome for the funeral. And something happened there — in the crowds, in the liturgy, in the sheer weight of two thousand years of intellectual and spiritual tradition made visible — that completed a journey we'd been on for a long time. We entered the Catholic Church.

I spent the next two decades trying to build Catholic intellectual life in Canada. I helped establish three Catholic Studies programs. I served as Academic Dean at Newman Theological College in Edmonton, where we doubled enrollment and secured over a million dollars in outreach funding. I published eleven books on Catholic education and culture. I was honoured with the Queen Elizabeth II Platinum Jubilee Medal.

And through all of it, one thing became painfully clear: **Canada has almost no institutional infrastructure for Catholic intellectual and cultural life.** The United States has the Napa Institute, the Acton Institute, FOCUS, Word on Fire, the Augustine Institute, and dozens of others. Canada has... almost nothing.

The Gregory the Great Institute exists to change that.

We are building an institution — not a project, not a program, but an institution — that will form Catholic leaders, publish serious scholarship, and cultivate the kind of community that can sustain cultural renewal over decades. We named it after St. Gregory the Great because Gregory did exactly this in the sixth century: he rebuilt civilization from the ruins of the Roman Empire through liturgy, education, and pastoral care.

In our first year, we published *Magnalia* — our journal of Catholic cultural renewal — and placed it in the hands of every Catholic bishop and seminary rector in Canada. We ran formation programs in four provinces. We hosted the inaugural Renewing Culture Conference. We built a community of over 1,500 people who believe, as we do, that Catholic intellectual life in Canada is worth rebuilding.

I'm glad you're here. The Magnalia Letter is one small part of this work — but an important one. The ideas that arrive in your inbox each month are drawn from the same tradition that changed our lives in Rome twenty years ago.

If anything in these emails resonates, I'd love to hear from you. Just reply — I read every response.

God bless,

Ryan

## Lead Magnet: Reading List

### A Reading List for Renewing Canadian Catholic Culture
*Curated by Dr. Ryan Topping*

**Format:** PDF, designed in GGI brand (navy/gold/parchment), 4-6 pages
**Delivery:** Download link in Email 1 + landing page

#### Suggested Structure

**Introduction** (half page)
A short note from Dr. Topping — why these books matter, what they share in common, and how they connect to the Institute's mission. Written in first person, warm and intellectual.

**The List** (10 books, half page each)

Each entry:
- Title, Author, Year
- 3-4 sentence personal recommendation (not a review — why *this* book, why *now*, what it will do for the reader)
- One pull quote from the book

**Suggested titles** (to be confirmed/edited by Ryan):

1. *Leisure: The Basis of Culture* — Josef Pieper
   (The Conference theme. Why culture requires leisure, and why leisure requires worship.)

2. *The Abolition of Man* — C.S. Lewis
   (The Book Study text. The clearest 80 pages ever written on why objective value matters.)

3. *The Everlasting Man* — G.K. Chesterton
   (The Catholic imagination at its most sweeping.)

4. *After Virtue* — Alasdair MacIntyre
   (Why moral philosophy collapsed and what the Benedict Option actually means.)

5. *Rebuilding Catholic Culture* — Ryan Topping
   (Ryan's own argument for how the catechism can shape common life.)

6. *The Case for Catholic Education* — Ryan Topping
   (Why Catholic schools matter — and what's gone wrong.)

7. *An Essay on the Development of Christian Doctrine* — John Henry Newman
   (The convert's companion. How doctrine grows without contradiction.)

8. *The Spirit of the Liturgy* — Joseph Ratzinger
   (Why worship is the source, not the ornament, of culture.)

9. *Orthodoxy* — G.K. Chesterton
   (The joy of thinking with the tradition.)

10. *Witness to Hope* — George Weigel
    (The biography of John Paul II that contextualizes everything GGI is trying to do.)

**Closing** (quarter page)
Brief note connecting the list back to the Institute. "If these books resonate with you, the Gregory the Great Institute exists to build the community and institutions that bring these ideas to life in Canada." Links to: Magnalia Letter, Book Studies, gregorythegreat.ca.

## Implementation Notes

### ActiveCampaign Setup

1. **List:** "Magnalia Letter Subscribers"
2. **Automation:** "Welcome Series" triggered on list subscription
3. **Wait steps:** Day 0 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14 → Day 21
4. **Conditions:**
   - Email 4: Branch by tag — `source:event:*` → Variant B, `source:lead-magnet:*` OR `source:youtube` → Variant C, else → Variant A
   - If contact has tag `donor:active` at any point, skip Email 5 (they've already converted)
   - If contact opens 0 of first 3 emails, add tag `engagement:cold` (for re-engagement later)
5. **Goal:** Contact clicks CTA in Email 5 OR is tagged `donor:active` → exits automation as "converted"
6. **Post-series:** Contact enters regular monthly Magnalia Letter cadence

### Tagging on Conversion
- Clicks "Subscribe to Magnalia" → tag `interest:magnalia-subscription`
- Clicks "Become a Friend" → tag `interest:friend-tier`
- These tags trigger Victor's weekly signal report

### Metrics to Track
- Open rate per email (benchmark: 40-50% for warm list)
- Click rate per email (benchmark: 8-12%)
- Email 5 conversion rate (benchmark: 3-5% for warm welcome series)
- Unsubscribe rate per email (flag if >1% on any single email)
- Overall series completion rate
