# Giving Structure — Operating Manual

**Gregory the Great Institute**
Last updated: April 2026

---

## Design Principles

1. **Simplicity over sophistication.** Every tier is a promise the team must deliver. A 3-person team cannot manage 5 tiers with 20+ distinct benefits. Three tiers + one aspirational signal is the maximum.

2. **Products, not donations.** Each tier is a named relationship — not a donation receipt. People buy something they want (a subscription, a community, a partnership), not charity.

3. **The top donors don't pick from a menu.** Leadership Circle and above are personal relationships with Dr. Topping. What they receive is bespoke — shaped in conversation, not codified on a webpage. Don't promise things publicly that lock you into delivering them.

4. **Round numbers.** $15, $50, $5,000, $25,000. Not $208. Round numbers signal confidence and intentionality.

5. **Lead with annual, soften with monthly.** For higher tiers, the annual figure is the anchor. The monthly equivalent helps people budget. "$2,500/year (~$200/month)" is better than "$208/month."

## The Three Tiers

### Friend of the Institute — $15+/month ($180+/year)

**Who it's for:** Anyone who believes in the mission and wants to participate. The broadest entry point.

**What the Institute delivers:**
- Monthly prayer inclusion
- Magnalia Letter (free curated monthly guide)
- Magnalia digital edition (PDF of each issue)
- Early event registration
- Named recognition on the website

**Admin burden:** Low. Prayer list update (monthly). PDF email (twice yearly). Website name list (quarterly update). Early event link (per event).

**Landing page:** `/support/friend/`

---

### Patron — $50+/month ($600+/year)

**Who it's for:** The backbone. People who want a tangible, ongoing relationship with the Institute and its work.

**What the Institute delivers:**
- Everything in Friend, plus:
- Magnalia print subscription (2 issues/year, mailed)
- Named recognition in Magnalia (printed in each issue)
- Signed copy of *Renewing Catholic Schools* (on joining)
- Annual Patron gathering with Dr. Topping (one event/year)
- Conference early-bird + 10% discount

**Admin burden:** Moderate. Mailing list for print Magnalia. One book shipped per new Patron. One annual gathering to organize. Name list for Magnalia print run. Conference discount tracking.

**Landing page:** `/magnalia/patron/`

**Important:** The Patron gathering is annual, not quarterly. Quarterly was too much for the team to deliver. One high-quality gathering per year is better than four mediocre ones.

---

### Leadership Circle — $5,000+/year (by conversation)

**Who it's for:** High-capacity donors who want strategic involvement. These are personal relationships, not tier subscribers.

**What the Institute delivers:**
- Everything in Patron, plus:
- Personal relationship with Dr. Topping
- Input on Institute direction (informal, not a board seat)
- Named recognition in flagship programs (if desired)
- Annual Leadership gathering
- Whatever else makes sense for the specific relationship

**Admin burden:** Low volume, high touch. Ryan manages these relationships personally. Expect 3–10 partners at this level. Each one is different.

**Landing page:** `/support/leadership/`

**What is NOT promised publicly:**
- ~~Seat on the Patron Advisory Council~~ — removed. Too much access for too many people. If Ryan wants someone's counsel, he asks them individually.
- ~~Quarterly strategic briefings~~ — removed. An annual gathering + personal relationship is enough. Quarterly commitments become admin overhead.
- ~~Co-naming of a major program~~ — available in conversation, but not promised as a tier benefit. Some partners want recognition; some want anonymity. Let Ryan navigate this per-person.

## Founding Partner — $25,000+/year (signal, not a tier)

**What it is:** A section on the Leadership Circle page that signals the Institute's capacity to receive and deploy transformative gifts. It is NOT a separate tier with its own benefit list.

**Why it exists:** High-capacity donors need to see that GGI thinks big enough to receive their big gift. Without this signal, the site caps perceived ambition at $5K.

**What it says:** "The Catholic trade school. Certificate programs. A national think-tank. These are the next phase — and they require founding partners."

**What happens when someone responds:** Ryan has a personal conversation. The partnership is shaped entirely in that conversation. No public promises to manage.

## Honor / Memorial Giving

**What it is:** A checkbox on the donation form, not a separate product or page.

**How it works:**
1. Donor indicates "In Honor of" or "In Memory of"
2. Donor provides honoree name
3. Donor optionally provides a name and email to notify
4. Institute sends a personal note to the designated person/family

**Admin burden:** One email per tribute gift. Can be automated via ActiveCampaign when forms are live.

**Location on site:** Section on `/support/donate/` page.

**Benchmarks:** This is standard practice. Hillsdale, FOCUS, Word on Fire, and most major nonprofits do exactly this — a form field, not a program.

## What We Deliberately Removed

| Removed | Reason |
|---------|--------|
| **Underwriter tier ($2,500/year)** | Created a 4th tier with distinct benefits the team had to track. The naming/underwriting opportunity is still available — through a Leadership Circle conversation, not a public tier. |
| **Founding Fellow as a standalone tier** | Replaced with "Founding Partner" as a signal section on the Leadership page. No public benefit list = no admin burden. |
| **Quarterly Patron Conversations** | Changed to annual gathering. Quarterly commitments become overhead for 3 staff. |
| **Advisory Council seat** | Removed from public promises. If Ryan wants counsel from a donor, he asks. This shouldn't be a tier perk. |
| **5-tier grid on support page** | Reduced to 3. Visitors were choosing between too many options. Research shows 3 options converts better than 5. |

## Comparable Organizations (Benchmarks)

| Organization | Model | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| **Hillsdale College** | President's Club + Legacy Society + Independence Club + tribute gifts | No complex tiers. Three named groups. Tribute = form checkbox. |
| **FOCUS** | Give / Support a Campus / Support a Missionary + Honor/Memorial | No tiers at all. Direct giving with direction options. |
| **Acton Institute** | Friends of Acton + tribute gifts | Simple recurring + tribute. High-capacity handled privately. |

**The pattern:** Small teams use 2–3 named levels publicly. Everything above that is a personal conversation. Nobody with a small team runs 5 named tiers with different benefit packages.

## Revenue Model (from Cultivating the Garden)

The giving structure is one piece of a larger audience development strategy. The full model:

```
Discovery → Capture → Nurture → Segment → Convert

Free content (YouTube, Library, Letter)
    → Email list (ActiveCampaign)
        → Product engagement (Letter, events, Book Studies)
            → First purchase (Magnalia subscription, event ticket)
                → Recurring relationship (Friend, Patron)
                    → High-capacity cultivation (Leadership, Founding Partner)
```

The website's job is Layers 1–3 (Discovery, Capture, Nurture). The giving structure is Layer 5 (Conversion). Victor's personal outreach fills the gaps — especially for Leadership prospects identified through CRM signals.

## When to Revisit This Structure

- **When you hit 50+ Patrons** — consider whether the annual gathering needs to split into regional events
- **When you hit 10+ Leadership partners** — consider whether the gathering needs its own format
- **When CRA charitable status is finalized** — update tax receipt language and explore Google Ad Grants
- **When online payments go live (Zeffy/Stripe)** — the donation form becomes real. Honor/memorial fields go live. Tier selection becomes automated.
- **When you hire a dedicated development person** — you may have capacity for more tiers. Don't add them until you do.
